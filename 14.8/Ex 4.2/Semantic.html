FAQ

What is Semantic HTML?

Semantic HTML refers to the use of HTML tags that provide meaning to the content they contain, improving both accessibility and search engine optimization (SEO).

What is the difference between <section> and <div>?

The <section> element is a semantic HTML tag that defines a section of content that typically contains a heading. It's used to group related content together. On the other hand, <div> is a generic container element that doesn't have any semantic meaning; itâ€™s used for styling or scripting purposes.

Why should I use semantic HTML tags?

Using semantic HTML tags helps search engines understand the structure and content of your webpage, improving SEO. It also enhances accessibility, making it easier for screen readers to navigate your site, and contributes to a more organized codebase.

What is the <article> tag used for?

The <article> tag is used to represent a self-contained piece of content that could be independently distributed or reused, such as a blog post, news article, or forum post. Each <article> element should be a complete piece of content that makes sense on its own.
